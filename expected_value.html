💰 What Value Should I Expect? 🐴

📈 Understanding expected value is crucial for making informed decisions, especially when it comes to betting on horse races. Expected value (EV) is the average outcome you can anticipate from a particular action if you were to repeat it many times.

The formula for expected value is: $$EV = (Probability \ of \ Winning \times Net \ Profit \ if \ Winning) + (Probability \ of \ Losing \times Cost \ of \ Bet)$$ 📊

For example, if you bet €10 on a horse with a 30% (0.3) chance of winning and the payout is €30 (net profit of €20), the expected value is: $$EV = (0.3 \times €20) + (0.7 \times -€10) = €6 - €7 = -€1$$ 💸

This negative expected value suggests that, on average, you would lose €1 for every €10 bet placed on this particular outcome over the long run. 📉

Identifying bets with a positive expected value is the key to potentially profitable betting. This requires careful analysis of the odds and the true probabilities of each horse winning. 🧐 Look for situations where the odds offered by bookmakers are higher than your own assessment of the horse's chances. 💡

Remember, even with positive expected value bets, there's no guarantee of winning every time. However, over a large number of bets, you are more likely to come out ahead. 🏆
